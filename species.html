Crocodilian Species

Explore the fascinating diversity of crocodile species from around the world. Each one is unique and plays an essential role in its ecosystem.

Nile Crocodile

Crocodylus niloticus

One of the largest species, found in Africa’s freshwater habitats.

Saltwater Crocodile

Crocodylus porosus

The largest living reptile, found in Southeast Asia and Australia.

American Crocodile

Crocodylus acutus

Found in warm coastal regions from South Florida to South America.

Mugger Crocodile

Crocodylus palustris

Native to India, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka. Prefers rivers and marshes.

Philippine Crocodile

Crocodylus mindorensis

Critically endangered and endemic to the Philippines.

Gharial

Gavialis gangeticus

Critically endangered, known for its long, narrow snout.

Cuban Crocodile

Crocodylus rhombifer

Found only in Cuba, known for its aggressiveness and speed.

West African Crocodile

Crocodylus suchus

Native to West Africa, primarily found in rivers and lakes.

Orinoco Crocodile

Crocodylus intermedius

Endemic to the Orinoco River in South America, critically endangered.

Siamese Crocodile

Crocodylus siamensis

Native to Southeast Asia, endangered due to habitat loss and hunting.

New Guinea Crocodile

Crocodylus novaeguineae

Found in New Guinea, a freshwater species with smaller size.

Dwarf Crocodile

Crocodylus Osteolaemus tetraspis

The dwarf crocodile is the smallest living freshwater crocodile species..

Indo-Pacific Crocodile

Crocodylus novaeguineae

Found across islands in the Indo-Pacific region, it is known for its size.

Black Crocodile

Crocodylus niloticus

A form of the Nile crocodile found in specific regions of Africa.
